Festivals:

Luau (11th) & Dance of the Moonlight Jellies (28th)

  • Luau will require an edible, uncooked item as a donation to the potluck. Item quality, base sell price, and edibility (healing effect) are the 3 determining factors.
  • Gold/Iridium Spring items that will result in the best response: Cheese, Milk, Cauliflower, Truffle, Rhubarb Wine, Strawberry Wine

Fall

Fall has 2 festivals and 8 birthdays. Blackberry season is from the 8th-11th of the season.

Festivals:

Stardew Valley Fair (16th) & Spirit's Eve (27th)

  • Fair will require up to 9 items to put in the grange display. Display will be judged based on quality, sell price, and diversity of items. Items may be retrieved after.
  • Eight categories of items recognized by the fair: Animal Products; Artisan Goods; Cooking; Fish; Foraging, Flowers, & Tree Saps; Fruits; Minerals; Vegetables

Winter

Winter has 3 festivals and 9 birthdays.

Festivals:

Festival of Ice (8th), Night Market (15th-17th) & Feast of the Winter Star (25th)

  • The Night Market has a submarine that takes you underwater fishing. The submarine is the only way to catch Midnight Squid, Spook Fish, and Blobfish.
  • Can also catch: Seaweed, Sea Cucumber, Octopus, Super Cucumber, and a rare chance to catch a Pearl.
  • The solution for the Night Market's Mermaid Boat is: (left to right) 1-5-4-2-3.
    You can obtain a pearl here, but only once per player per saved game.
  • There will be a Secret Santa even for Feast of the Winter Star. You will need to bring a gift for the person assigned to you in your invitation letter.

Birthdays:
